Catalogue of Contemporary Reality Distortions
Y.R.Pan
Foreword

It’s common knowledge that the deadly mushroom is vividly colored and spotted. But what eludes most is that the deadliest of all mushrooms appears just like the everyday grocery mushrooms. Likewise, the threat to benevolence may come in part from the apparent, but not in any lesser part from the subtle, for those lurking in the dark may practice malevolence with impunity and without measure. Nor is every reality distortion created by an apparent act of sophistry or blitheful ignorance. Certain are attended with subtle eidolae. These eidolae are often disguised as one’s own thoughts, slithering into the inner reaches of the mind, and impressing it with subliminal effects on the basis of spurious notions, which, once taken root, become prejudicial to the host and may spread from one host to another by means of subsequent eidola emission.

“Artificial Intelligence is the magic conch”
Verdict

Reality does not meet the hype.

Notes

The masses are wont to jump onto the AI hype wagon out of ignorance: ignorant of the AI’s inner workings, such as the underlying learning algorithms and decision trees that dictate its behaviors and outputs. And this ignorance begets faith and fantasy in the actual capabilities of AI. As WSJ and industry insiders pointed out, even the best-known AIs out there can frequently make a spectrum of errors ranging from unable to solve basic math questions in natural language when phrased in a certain way; unable to classify filterable content correctly; to even getting basic facts wrong (e.g. the row number of CW in excel, the chapter of a book with a certain quote, the safe path in games where order or choices matter, travel requirements of a certain country, &c). While some errors may be the result of learning gaps, much of the error is attributed to the fact that it generally depends on webscraping for answers, and these answers they scraped or synthesized tend to be erroneous, in part or whole, because most information on the web are frivolous and specious (On the value of information); for they are not able to reliably identify and separate out the dross before synthesizing them. To put simply, AI is a great helper or advisor, especially for quick reference or summary of certain information; but this utility ought not be construed in the absolute sense. Double checking with official/ authoritative sources or domain knowledge remains a necessary due diligence. Also worth nothing is that AI doesn’t create, it recycles. Nearly every photo, video and textual content it “creates” is but a remix of those that already existed somewhere else. It’s analogous to Epicurus’ eidolae, in that everything humans can imagine or picture in their minds are but the reflection or remix of the things that already exist, as these things emit eidola (singular or composite). Humans cannot imagine something outside of the sandbox of defined existence. For example, the common notion of an alien has eyes, head and limbs (features that already exist in humans or other living creatures), the pagan gods are but the remixes of existing animal body parts (eagle, dog, lion, elephant…), and so forth.
